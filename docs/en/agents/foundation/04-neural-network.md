## Learning Path

### Step 1: Start with Moving Yao
The first thing Brother Sheng touched was the activation function.
"Moving Yao is the moment of change, just like a switch being activated, being turned on."

What activation function does:
- ● Judge whether the signal is strong enough
- ● Strong enough → Activate (Yang Yao), pass it down
- ● Not strong enough → Not activate (Yin Yao), stay silent

Moving Yao is the moment when Yin changes to Yang.

### Step 2: Layer-by-Layer Hexagram Change = Layer-by-Layer Transmission
"In Yijing, we pay attention to the appearance of the hexagram after the moving Yao changes to interpret the hexagram.
The machine uses moving Yao to take Yang Yao as the result and as the parameter for the next layer."

$$Original Hexagram \xrightarrow{Moving Yao Activation} Changed Hexagram \xrightarrow{Moving Yao Activation} Changed Hexagram \xrightarrow{} \cdots \xrightarrow{} Final Result$$

The output of each layer is the input of the next layer.
The hexagram after change becomes the original hexagram of the next layer.

### Step 3: Dao gives birth to One, One gives birth to Two, Two gives birth to Three
Brother Sheng saw the underlying logic of neural network from the root:

| Progression of Dao | Neural Network Correspondence |

Computer science and Yijing share the same root: Yin-Yang.

### Step 4: Activation is not just a switch, but a degree
It's not just 0 or 1,
but all possibilities between $$0.0$$ and $$1.0$$.

It's not just Yin Yao or Yang Yao,
but the position, strength, and relationship with other Yaos.
The Yang in the first Yao has different meaning from the Yang in the top Yao.
Degree determines meaning.

### Step 5: Middleware
Brother Sheng used a precise term — middleware.
Not the starting point, not the end point,
but the thing that allows signals to flow, transform, and transmit.

Each layer of the neural network is exactly middleware —
not the answer, but the channel that allows the answer to grow step by step.

**Yijing Mapping**: Mutual Hexagram —
Not the original hexagram, not the changed hexagram,
but the structure hidden in the middle that allows change to happen.
