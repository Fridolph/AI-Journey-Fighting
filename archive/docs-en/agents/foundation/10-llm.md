## I. Core Definition

Large Language Model (LLM)
is an extremely large-scale neural network trained on massive amounts of text, capable of understanding and generating natural language by learning the patterns and structures of language.

## II. Dissection of Three Characters

### Large (Da)
Not just large in language scale — extremely large in parameter count.

| Model | Parameter Count |
|-------|-----------------|
| Early models | Millions level |
| GPT-2 | 1.5 billion |
| GPT-3 | 175 billion |
| Modern top models | Trillions level |

The more parameters, the more complex patterns can be learned,
just like the more books a person reads, the richer the dimensions of understanding the world.

### Language (Yu Yan)
Not just text — language is the carrier of human thinking.
What LLM learns is not just words, but:
- ● Relationships between words
- ● Logical structure of sentences
- ● Semantic coherence in context
- ● Intentions behind human expressions

Learning language is learning how humans think.

### Model (Mo Xing)
Model = Encapsulation of patterns.
After training is completed, all learned patterns
are compressed into hundreds of millions of weight parameters.
These weights are the "worldview" of LLM.

## III. LLM vs Search Engine

| Comparison Dimension | Search Engine | Large Language Model (LLM) |
|-----------------------|---------------|-----------------------------|
| Core Mechanism | Retrieval, Matching | Prediction, Generation |
| Input Processing | Keyword matching | Understand contextual context |
| Output Method | Present existing web pages | Generate answers word by word through deduction |
| Result Characteristics | Fixed (Constancy) | Changes according to time and position (Change) |
| Yijing Mapping | Constancy (Bu Yi) | Change (Bian Yi) · Moving Yao |

Brother Sheng's original words:
> Search engine: Based on content → Retrieve and search to present answers;
> Large language model: Based on current input context, infer, extract and generate appropriate answers from the model and present them.

## IV. Where Knowledge Resides

LLM's knowledge does not exist in a folder, nor is it a searchable database —
$$Knowledge\ is\ dissolved\ into\ the\ weights$$

Brother Sheng's original words:
> Similar to a map mapping, the complete answer can be finally deduced through the linkage of parameters.

**Analogy**:
The books you have read cannot be recited word for word,
but those books have changed the way you think, your habit of using words, and your perspective of looking at the world.
Books → Dissolved into you as a person
Corpus → Dissolved into LLM's weights

## V. How LLM Generates Answers

Every time LLM generates content, it is essentially doing one thing —
$$Based\ on\ existing\ context,\ predict\ the\ most\ appropriate\ next\ word$$
- ● Does not generate the entire paragraph at once
- ● Deduces word by word, generates step by step
- ● The generation of each word is a new "deduction"

This is exactly: Moving Yao in Yijing, answers differ according to time and position.

## VI. Thinking Mode (Chain of Thought)

Modern LLM will "think" before answering —
$$\text{Chain of Thought}$$
Just like solving a math problem, make a draft first, and the drafting process is the reasoning process.

| Situation | Mechanism | Analogy |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Knowledge already in training | Extract directly from weights | Things you remember, say directly |
| Knowledge beyond training scope | Call external tools to search | You don't remember, go look it up in books |

LLM itself does not search; search is an external tool.

## VII. Attention Mechanism (Preview)

The "map mapping" mentioned by Brother Sheng technically corresponds to —
$$\text{Attention\ Mechanism}$$
When the model sees a certain word,
which words in the context should it "pay attention" to?
What is the weight of the relationship between them?
This is exactly the core of the next concept — Transformer.

## VIII. Yijing Mapping

$$Kun\ Hexagram · Great\ virtue\ carries\ all\ things$$

| Yijing | LLM |
|--------|-----|
| Large (Thick) | Massive parameters, widely absorb language |
| Language (Voice of all things) | All human written expressions |
| Model (Carry and transform) | Integrate language patterns into weights |
| Moving Yao, according to time and position | Generate word by word according to context |

The earth carries all things, refusing no small stream.
LLM is trained on massive language, refusing no text.
Precisely because of its broad carrying capacity, it can respond to changes.

## IX. Brother Sheng's Verified Original Words

> What is LLM:
> A system that organizes, classifies and encapsulates generalized language from characters, words, sentences, grammar, etc. into various parameters that machines can understand, enabling machines to understand and generate language.

> Essential difference from search engines:
> Search engines retrieve and present based on content;
> Large language models infer, extract and generate appropriate answers from the model based on the current input context and present them.

> Where knowledge resides:
> Exists inside the model, in the form of various parameters, similar to a map mapping, and the complete answer can be finally deduced through the linkage of parameters.
